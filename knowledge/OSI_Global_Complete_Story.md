Company Overview
================

OSI Global is a Gartner-recognized IT infrastructure solutions provider
headquartered in Santa Barbara, California at 606 Olive Street. The
company operates across four core service pillars: Hardware, Optics and
Open Line Systems, Professional Services, and Systain Third-Party
Maintenance. With offices in San Francisco, Los Angeles, Phoenix,
Dallas, Denver, New York, Sacramento, and Amsterdam, OSI delivers global
reach with local service excellence.

OSI\'s official tagline is \"Your IT infrastructure, optimized.\" The
company is recognized for its consultative approach, working as trusted
advisors throughout design, deployment, and support rather than simply
delivering hardware. Client testimonials consistently emphasize
responsiveness, flexibility, and the ability to handle logistical and
cultural nuances across multi-country projects, including deployments in
regions such as Peru and other international locations.

The company saves customers 50-80% off hardware and support pricing from
leading OEMs while maintaining enterprise-grade quality and reliability.

Pillar 1: Hardware
==================

Networking Equipment
--------------------

OSI is a leading independent network hardware provider offering
customized new and refurbished solutions across all major OEMs. The
networking portfolio includes Cisco, Juniper, Aruba (HP), Fortinet,
Brocade, F5, and Dell, encompassing switches, routers, firewalls, SUP
engines, line cards, power supplies, and accessories. OSI helps extend
hardware lifecycle for equipment deemed End-of-Life by OEMs, giving
customers flexible options to delay capex spending while maintaining
network performance.

Servers and Storage
-------------------

OSI provides current generation, legacy, and customized server and
storage configurations across all major platforms with full maintenance
support. Enterprise server platforms include IBM pSeries, iSeries, and
xSeries; SUN/Oracle x86 and UNIX-Solaris; HPE ProLiant; Dell PowerEdge;
Cisco UCS; and Supermicro. SAN and storage capabilities span EMC VNX,
VMAX, Isilon, and Data Domain; NetApp FAS and E-Series; IBM XIV,
Storwize, and D/N Series; HDS AMS, USP, VSP, and HUS; HPE 3PAR, Nimble,
MSA, StoreEasy, and StoreOnce; and Infinidat. SAN switching includes
Brocade 4, 8, and 16GB switching and directors.

Enterprise Memory (DIMMs)
-------------------------

As memory demand grows, OSI helps organizations stretch their
infrastructure by offering high-performance DIMMs at a fraction of OEM
pricing. The company provides enterprise-grade memory modules across all
major OEM platforms including Dell, HPE, Cisco UCS, and Supermicro,
supporting capacities from 16GB through 32GB, 64GB, 128GB, and 256GB.
OSI offers both OEM-branded and approved (OEM-equivalent) DIMMs.
Approved DIMMs are functionally identical without the logo and deliver
substantial savings, routinely 80-90% off list price. A 64GB DIMM
listing at \$6,000 can be supplied at a fraction of that cost. All DIMMs
go through a validation process with sourcing from top-tier
manufacturers including Samsung, Micron, and Hynix. Systems built by OSI
include fully tested memory with logged reports. OSI has fulfilled
same-day shipments of over 6,000 DIMMs and maintains deep inventory
across multiple generations for near-immediate turnaround on large-scale
upgrades.

Surplus and Legacy Equipment
----------------------------

OSI maintains ready inventory of factory-sealed, pre-owned, and legacy
hardware, reducing long lead times and enabling faster deployment and
scalability for high-demand projects.

Pillar 2: Optics and Open Line Systems
======================================

Optical Transceivers
--------------------

OSI offers a full range of pluggable optical transceivers for rates from
100MB to 800G in all applicable form factors including QSFP, QSFP-DD,
CFP, XFP, and SFP. Transceivers are available for Grey, CWDM, and DWDM
applications and are coded in-house with every OEM platform supported
across 40+ platforms. The company also provides custom-build solutions
that OEMs will not or cannot offer, including 10G copper optics,
extended distances beyond OEM specifications, twinax cables for
multi-platform environments (such as Cisco-coded on one side and
Intel-coded on the other), DWDMs coded as SFP+ ER and ZR for platforms
that do not accept DWDM technology, and 100G CFP ERs coded as LRs. Cable
offerings include simplex, duplex, copper patch cables, and MPO/MTP with
custom builds and custom lengths.

Smartoptics Partnership
-----------------------

OSI is the largest global partner for Smartoptics, delivering advanced
optical networking solutions. All OSI optics are certified in
Smartoptics platforms. The partnership delivers solutions including 800G
transponders for high-throughput AI and data center needs. Smartoptics
product families offered through OSI include the DCP-M active open line
systems for point-to-point links with automatic optical setup, the DCP-R
family for ROADM-based ring and mesh networks compliant with OpenROADM
API, the DCP-F family for maximum configuration flexibility in compact
form factors, transponder platforms including the DCP-1203 for 100G/400G
transport and the DCP-108 eight-channel open transponder, and the
SoSmart Software Suite for SDN-based open network management.

Passive Optical Networking
--------------------------

The passive optical portfolio includes CWDM and DWDM multiplexers and
OADM modules supporting up to 18 CWDM and 80 DWDM simultaneous channels
over dark fiber pairs. Products serve applications including 100G/10G
Ethernet, SDH/SONET, Fibre Channel/FICON, FTTx, and CATV. The H-Series
platform provides high-density, cost-efficient passive optical layer
nodes with up to five times higher packaging density than previous
solutions.

Open Line Systems
-----------------

OSI provides custom-build open line system solutions including
point-to-point WDM systems, passive filters, in-line amplification, and
100G/400G DWDM. In an open optical network, DWDM transceivers may be
embedded directly into switches and routers with output signals
multiplexed onto fiber by passive filters or active open line systems,
resulting in disaggregated architectures with much better
price-to-performance ratios.

The team has proven experience coordinating multi-country projects
including logistics, language coordination, and deployment support, with
the ability to mobilize virtually anywhere in the world.

Pillar 4: Systain Third-Party Maintenance
=========================================

Systain is OSI Global\'s world-class third-party maintenance service for
networking and server-storage assets, accessible at systain.io. Systain
provides 24x7x365 TAC (Technical Assistance Center) coverage as
standard, with Level 2 and Level 3 engineers answering all calls for
faster escalation compared to OEM support queues.

Hardware replacement SLAs include Next Business Day, 4-Hour, and 4-Hour
with technician onsite options, all with global coverage. Systain
supports End-of-Life products that OEMs no longer maintain and offers a
hybrid strategy that provides coverage where traditional VARs cannot.

Contracts are break/fix in nature, covering hardware issues from routine
equipment usage. The centralized contract and ticket management portal
allows clients to manage multi-vendor maintenance agreements under one
roof. Systain\'s tagline is \"Better service, better options, better
price. Experience the OSI difference.\"

Sales Messaging Guidelines
==========================

Lead with Industry Credibility
------------------------------

Open presentations by emphasizing Gartner recognition and strategic
partnership endorsements. This establishes trust before getting into
product details.

Feature Case-Based Proof
------------------------

Include real-world anecdotes such as the international deployment in
Peru to demonstrate logistical coordination and client support
strengths. Concrete examples resonate more than capability lists.

Position Inventory Readiness as Strategic Advantage
---------------------------------------------------

Highlight how fast access to in-stock Smartoptics optical modules
including 800G transponders can differentiate clients\' rollout
timelines. Same-day fulfillment capability on items like DIMMs (6,000+
units shipped same-day) is a compelling proof point.

Emphasize Consultative Value
----------------------------

OSI does not just deliver hardware. The team works as trusted advisors
throughout design, deployment, and support. This consultative approach
is a key differentiator against commodity resellers.

Adapt Per Vertical
------------------

Tailor messaging to specific industries. Telco carriers value uptime and
global consistency. Hyperscalers prioritize scale and cost efficiency.
Service providers need flexibility and rapid deployment. Financial
services firms require reliability and compliance awareness.
