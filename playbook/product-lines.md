# OSI Product Lines

**Last updated:** 2026-06-05

Evaluate ALL of these on every prospect. Do not skip compute/components for network engineers — DIMMs and servers come up constantly.

---

## 1. Optics — SmartOptics Transceivers

**What:** SFP/QSFP transceivers, OSI private-labeled (manufactured by SmartOptics). Single-mode, multi-mode, all speeds.

**Opening wedge:** Free sample. Any network engineer or architect is worth a sample offer. It removes all friction from the first conversation.

**Who to target:** Network engineers, architects, infrastructure leads. Anyone managing switches, routers, or fiber.

**Lead line:** "We send a complimentary box of SmartOptics SFPs — compatible with your existing gear — so you can test before you buy anything."

---

## 2. DWDM and Open Line Systems — SmartOptics DCP Platform

**What:** Open, vendor-agnostic DWDM platform. Complements Infinera, Ciena, Nokia. 30-50% below Ciena and Nokia on price. Ships in days to weeks, not months.

**Who to target:** Regional fiber ops, ISPs, broadband providers, carriers, anyone dealing with 100G/400G wave extension. Cell site backhaul (CSBH) is a strong secondary angle.

**Pain to lead with:** Lead time on transport gear (hyperscalers and neo-cloud providers are consuming supply). Wave extension costs on existing backbone. Backbone capacity pressure from AI-driven bandwidth demand.

**Proof point:** "Your peers are using Smartoptics as their hammer — everything is a nail." (See winning-emails/ for the full fiber operator opener.)

**Competitive note:** Positions against Ciena and Nokia. OSI IS a Nokia authorized partner — never position Nokia negatively if the prospect uses Nokia.

---

## 3. Compute and Components — Dell/HP Servers + DIMMs

**What:** Dell and HP servers (OSI is an authorized partner for both). DIMMs: Samsung, Hynix, Micron — manufacturer warranties, priced below OEM.

**Sequencing rule:** Lead with DIMMs first. Server refresh is the second conversation once trust is established.

**Who to target:** Anyone with "servers," "hardware," "storage," or "data center" in their title or skills. Do not skip this for network-only titles — they often know who buys compute.

**Pain to lead with:** OEM memory pricing, server refresh cycles post-COVID backlog, AI infrastructure build-outs driving DIMM demand.

---

## 4. Storage — Pre-Owned NetApp + Enterprise Storage

**What:** Pre-owned NetApp and enterprise storage arrays. OSI TPM available on all units.

**Who to target:** Storage admins, infrastructure managers, data center ops, anyone running NAS/SAN at scale.

**Pain to lead with:** NetApp renewal costs, capacity gaps, refresh budget constraints.

---

## 5. TPM — Third-Party Maintenance

**What:** Gartner-recognized TPM. 40-60% below OEM support. Multi-vendor: Cisco, Dell, HP, NetApp, Juniper, Arista. Privately owned, no PE.

**Who to target:** IT directors, VP infrastructure, procurement leads. Anyone renewing Cisco SmartNet or Dell ProSupport.

**Important:** OSI is NOT a Cisco partner. Cannot provide SmartNet or DNA licensing. TPM only for Cisco gear — support and maintenance, not licensing.

**Pain to lead with:** SmartNet renewal sticker shock. "We typically save clients 40-60% on the same coverage — on the same gear — with a Gartner-recognized provider."

---

## 6. Networking Gear — Pre-Owned + New

**What:**
- Pre-owned Cisco, Juniper, Arista (tested, OSI TPM available)
- New Nokia (authorized partner)
- New Aruba, Fortinet

**Who to target:** Network engineers doing refresh or expansion. Anyone who mentions lifecycle, EoL gear, or budget constraints on new gear.

---

## 7. Professional Services

**What:** Deployment, design, migration.

**Rule:** Never lead cold with services. Second or third conversation only, after a hardware or maintenance relationship is established.
