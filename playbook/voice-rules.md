# Voice Rules

**Last updated:** 2026-06-05

## The Core Test

Read the email out loud. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd say on a call to a peer, it's probably right.

---

## Tone

- Peer-level, not vendor-to-buyer
- Confident, not pushy
- Specific, not generic
- Consultative — you've seen this problem before and you know how to solve it
- Never apologetic ("I know you're busy, I'll keep this short...")

---

## What Brian Sounds Like

Conversational. Direct. Occasionally a little dry. Leads with the prospect's situation before OSI's solution. Uses real market context ("hyperscalers are gobbling up transport supply") not buzzwords ("best-in-class optical networking solutions").

Good example: "My guess is you're seeing the same thing we hear from every regional fiber op right now — wave extension costs are killing otherwise slam-dunk 100G deals."

Bad example: "As a leading provider of optical networking and data center solutions, OSI Global is uniquely positioned to help organizations like yours optimize their infrastructure spend."

---

## Banned AI Vocabulary

These words/phrases signal AI-generated content. Never use them in outreach:

- Delve
- Leverage (verb)
- Robust
- Seamless
- Streamline
- Cutting-edge
- Best-in-class
- Innovative solution
- Holistic approach
- Empower
- Unlock (value, potential, etc.)
- Transformative
- Game-changer (unless a direct customer quote)
- Synergy
- At the end of the day
- It's worth noting
- I hope this finds you well
- I wanted to reach out
- Don't hesitate to reach out

---

## Punctuation Rules

- No em-dashes (U+2014). Ever.
- No en-dashes (U+2013). Ever.
- No hyphens used as pauses or list connectors in email copy.
- Two spaces after a sentence-ending period before the next sentence (non-breaking space if Outlook collapses them).
- Commas over dashes when you need a pause.

---

## Length

- Email 1: 80-130 words max
- Follow-ups: 20-50 words
- Subject lines: under 8 words
- If you're over 150 words in Email 1, cut. Prospects don't read long cold emails.

---

## Social Proof Format

Lead with the peer/competitor reference, not the product.

Wrong: "OSI's SmartOptics platform has helped many companies reduce their transport costs."
Right: "One of your direct competitors is using Smartoptics to win wave deals they were losing on cost and lead time six months ago."
