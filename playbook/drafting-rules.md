# Drafting Rules

**Last updated:** 2026-06-05

## The Absolute Rules (never break)

- **No em-dashes (U+2014) or en-dashes (U+2013).** Ever. Split the thought or use a comma.
- **No hyphens used as pauses.** "We offer servers, optics, and TPM" — not "servers - optics - TPM."
- **One blank line between last sentence and Best,.** Not zero. Not two. One.
- **Never type Best, or Brian at the bottom.** Outlook signature handles it.
- **No RE: in a subject unless it is a genuine reply thread from Sent Items.**

## Voice

Short sentences. Active voice. Lead with the prospect's world, not OSI's products. The prospect should feel like you've been watching their industry, not reading a product sheet.

Wrong: "OSI Global offers a full suite of optical networking and data center solutions including..."
Right: "My guess is you're seeing the same thing we hear from every regional fiber op right now..."

## Structure

- **Email 1:** One specific pain or market observation. One question or offer. Under 120 words.
- **Email 2:** "Any thoughts?" — reply thread, 3-5 words max.
- **Email 3:** New angle, different product line or different pain. Social proof if available.
- **Email 4:** Peer/competitor reference. "One of your competitors is doing X."
- **Email 5:** "Any thoughts?" reply again, or a short pivot.
- **Email 6:** Soft breakup. Leave the door open. No guilt.
- **Email 7:** Final. Different format (question only, or one-liner).

## Banned Phrases

- "Worth 15 minutes this week to compare?"
- "I wanted to reach out"
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "I'd love to connect"
- "Touching base"
- "Circle back"
- "Synergy"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Seamless"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "At the end of the day"
- "Game-changer" (unless quoting a customer directly)

## Subject Line Rules

- Under 8 words
- No "Re:" unless it's a real reply
- No ALL CAPS
- Questions work ("400G timing question", "quick question on branch connectivity")
- Specific beats generic ("Calix-compatible transceivers" beats "optical networking question")

## Swag Sequence (Emails 1-3 when using aaa-hs-7step-swag)

Email 1-3 are address-confirm touches only. Subject: "Re: Confirming address." Body confirms you're sending a complimentary SmartOptics SFP sample. Do not pitch product. Do not use swag emails as a vehicle for value prop — that's what emails 4-7 are for.
