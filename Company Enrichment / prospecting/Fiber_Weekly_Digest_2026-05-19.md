## 1. BEAD / Federal Fiber Programs

- **BEAD approvals essentially complete.** 54 of 56 states/territories have NTIA Final Proposal approval; NIST approved 52, releasing grant funds. NTIA hit its May target for full approval completion.
- **First BEAD subscriber went live May 14.** Nebraska's Vistabeam turned on the nation's first BEAD-funded connection — a fixed wireless link northwest of Ogallala delivering 800+ Mbps down, 200+ Mbps up. Louisiana also went live the same week.
- **Nebraska subgrant breakdown:** $44M provisionally awarded — fiber (Midstates Data Transport, 423 locations), fixed wireless (AMG Technology, 7,454 locations). Only ~11% of Nebraska's $405M total BEAD allocation provisionally awarded so far — more rounds coming.
- **House restored ReConnect funding for FY26** ($50.75M). Senate cleared ag bill with $109M for rural broadband.
- **Conexon Connect (Louisiana)** became first in the nation to receive an actual BEAD disbursement in February — $9M award, building in 6 northern Louisiana parishes, targeting 60,000 homes across 20 parishes. They also carry $215M+ in provisional BEAD awards across 8 states.

**OSI Angle:** States actively subgranting = ISPs and co-ops getting funded and entering procurement now. Nebraska, Louisiana, and any state with approved subgrants are actively buying optics and networking gear.
